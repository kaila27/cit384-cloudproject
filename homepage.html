Different Kinds of Clouds

☁ Cumulus Clouds ☁

Cumulus clouds usually have a puffy, cotton-ball like appearance. However, they have flat bases.

Cumulonimbus aka The Rain Cloud

Considered the “cousin” of the cumulus cloud, these are considered to be the most dangerous cloud type. Cumulonimbus can form alone, in clusters, or along cold front squall lines. These clouds are capable of producing lightning and other dangerous severe weather, such as tornadoes and hailstones.

How Cumulus Clouds are Formed

All cumulus clouds develop because of convection. When we have cool warm weather, the damp air rises and the water particles cools down and condensates into extremely big tiny water droplets. As the process continues, the water droplets accumulate upwards, creating the clouds we can recognize as white fluffy cumulus clouds.
